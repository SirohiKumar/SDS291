contrasts of coefficients


bat_energy = Sleuth2::case1002

a contrast is a weighted linear combination of the models coefficients which sum to 0. our models 4 existing coefficients are:

$$
\operatorname{\widehat{log(Energy)}} = -1.58 + 0.81(\operatorname{\log(Mass)}) + 0.1(\operatorname{Type}_{\operatorname{non-echolocating\ birds}}) + 0.08(\operatorname{Type}_{\operatorname{echolocating\ bats}})
$$

we want to contrast the 3rd and 4th coefficients (Typenon-echolocating birds and Typeecholocating-bats).

the general formula for a contrast is: \[g = C_0\hat{\beta}_0 + C_1\hat{\beta}_1 + ... + C_k\hat{\beta}_k\]

so if we give coefficients \(\hat{\beta}_0\) and \(\hat{\beta}_1\) weights of 0, \(\hat{\beta}_3\) a weight of 1, and \(\hat{\beta}_4\) a counterweight of \(-1\): \[g = (0 \cdot -1.58) + (0 \cdot 0.81) + (1 \cdot 0.1) + (-1 \cdot 0.08)\] \[g = 0.01 - 0.08\] \[g = 0.023\]

to determine if \(g\)s value is unusual, we can test it in a hypothesis test. however, this requires access to \(g\)s standard error. the variance of a contrast is the sum of each individual coefficients variance, plus negative two times the covariance of the two coefficients. the square root of this value is \(g\)s \(SE\). this means we need to generate a co/variance matrix from our model:


co_variance_matrix = vcov(bat_model)

the variance of each coefficient is on the diagonal, and the covariance between each pair is on the off-diagonals:


var_b3 = co_variance_matrix[3, 3]
var_b4 = co_variance_matrix[4, 4]
cov_b3_b4 = co_variance_matrix[3, 4]

var_g = var_b3 + var_b4 + -2*(cov_b3_b4)
var_g
[1] 0.02483792

take the square root of this value to find the standard error of \(g\):


SE_g = sqrt(var_g)
SE_g
[1] 0.1576005

construct the test statistic


## difference between (NE birds and NE bats) - {E bats and NE bats}
g = coef(bat_model)[3] - coef(bat_model)[4]

t = g / SE_g
